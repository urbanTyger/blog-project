The failings of the Journey

Thomas Miller Dec. 18, 2020
  • life
  • living
  • failing

We have all potentially done it; planned a journey that would be a zero elevation, perfect temperature, “easy” trip. We have estimated the time down to the second, along with all the supplies needed. It all looks so great, before we even start walking. If you are lucky, most times that trip goes exactly as planned, and when you get home, you have nothing but good memories of the time passed.

Failure and success go hand in hand

But sometimes after going only about 1 km, the steps get harder. It gets harder to simply go into the direction planned. This can be due to traffic, weather, health or numerous other reasons. Life’s journey, is simply sometimes like that.

Failure is the first step

What I have come to accept as an old man is a lesson I am still learning. That “trip” in life you want to take toward a certain goal, does not usually go according to plan. The cool part of that fact is that it is perfectly okay. This is the normal way even with experience, which should help minimize those parts of the journey being more difficult than planned.

When do we learn the most? I guess it is better to ask it in another way. When do we forge new information into our head so that it stays there? I would say this is when things are not going smoothly. Smooth and easy makes the mind go into auto-pilot. You do not have to pay much attention because there is not a full thought needed to continue forward. But as soon as you trip and fall on your face with dirt and ants in your mouth, do you realize what you missed. That realization helps cement in your mind the stone, root, leaf or whatever made you fall. If you ever walk the same path again, you will more than hopefully look out for it.

Learning is getting back up

So when we translate this to a journey in life, as a career or task, we can easily correlate the journey above to it. Trying something new does not come always as second nature to me. I try to plan it out to account for EVERY SINGLE ISSUE that could arise, no matter how improbable. This leads me to most of the time not even attempt my plan. Unlike the Mandalorian, this is not the way.

In order to get to the goal of success, I have to accept the failure and get over it. I need to learn from it and try again, and again and again. Each time, getting closer to my goal and learning immensely on my way. If I find my goal was wrong, modify it based on what I have learned, but still go towards.

Success comes after the first two parts are complete

If you keep repeating the learn-fail-learn-fail cycle, it will be disheartening. Those are the parts that stop success from being achieved. Sometimes there are valid reasons to stop and throw in the towel, but most time there are only more reasons to try again differently.

I am on this goal now, and I hope that after learning Web Development, along with all the “trips” I had on my FIRST project, I will still succeed in my end goal:

A New job!!!

(Preferably in a company that I like, and likes me)
